# Original Route Rejection Screen

Date: 2026-07-12

Status: no unconditional RH route was found in this screen.  Four proposed
lower mechanisms were tested and rejected before Lean API work.  The reusable
cutoff-free Schur probe is `046_cutoff_schur_flow_probe.py`.  RH remains
unproved.

## 1. Cutoff Canonical-System Flow

Paper 3 of the `connes-cvs` series proves that every prime-power threshold in
the cutoff path `Q_N(u)`, `u=log(c)`, creates the negative rank-one velocity
jump

```text
Delta Q_N'(log q)
  = -2 Lambda(q)/(sqrt(q) log(q)) 11^T.
```

Its Krein interpretation is a positive boundary mass.  This suggests a
canonical-system proof only if the analytic motion between events also has a
sign which preserves the positive cone.

The independent closed-form probe rejects that condition.  Away from every
prime-power event, `Q_N''(u)` is already indefinite.  Representative
high-precision midpoint values are

```text
+-----+-----+-----------------------------+
| N   | u   | eigenvalue range of Q_N''   |
+-----+-----+-----------------------------+
| 2   | 1.5 | [-2.043, +11.41]            |
| 2   | 2.0 | [-0.958, +12.99]            |
| 3   | 2.0 | [-5.088, +14.56]            |
| 3   | 2.8 | [-1.118, +17.44]            |
+-----+-----+-----------------------------+
```

The inverse-matrix curvature is also indefinite, and the second derivative of
`log det Q_N` changes sign.  Positive prime boundary masses therefore do not
generate the complete path through a positive cutoff evolution.

## 2. Schur-Impedance Flow

The scalar

```text
s_N(u) = 1/<1,Q_N(u)^(-1)1>
```

preserves the full pole/Gamma/prime cancellation and stayed positive in all
tested cells.  It is not monotone or completely monotone:

```text
N=2: s'(2.8)>0, s'(3.1)<0,
N=2: s''(2.0)>0, s''(2.3)<0.
```

Moreover this is the universal all-ones/even direction of the prime event.
Yoshida's RH-closing obstruction is the odd sector.  Positivity of this
capacity scalar is therefore weaker than the required odd Weil positivity and
cannot close the route.

## 3. Total Positivity Of The Xi Kernel

The 2026 paper arXiv:2602.01248 proposes a `PF_infinity` logarithmic kernel and
a separate Mellin construction of `Xi`.  Its total-positivity proof uses the
claim that finite positive sums of `PF_infinity` functions remain
`PF_infinity`.  The displayed multilinear argument is invalid: it produces
determinants whose columns come from different kernels, while the definition
controls only determinants generated by one common kernel.  Positive sums do
not have that closure property in general.

Direct high-precision tests of the genuine Riemann--Xi Fourier kernel found no
negative minors through random orders two to four and confirmed strict log
concavity on the tested interval.  This does not rescue the route.  The genuine
kernel decays superexponentially in both directions, so its bilateral Laplace
transform is entire.  Schoenberg's classification permits an entire transform
for a nonzero `PF_infinity` density only in the zero-free Gaussian case; the
Riemann kernel is not Gaussian.  It therefore cannot be `PF_infinity`, and a
negative minor must occur at some higher order.

## 4. Uniform Jensen Hyperbolicity

Griffin--Ono--Rolen--Zagier and the effective refinement arXiv:1910.01227 prove
hyperbolicity of the degree-`d`, shift-`n` Xi Jensen polynomial once

```text
n >= c exp(d/2).
```

Verified zeta zeros also prove all-shift hyperbolicity up to one fixed, very
large degree.  These regions do not cover the plane: the ray `n=0`,
`d -> infinity` remains, and its complete hyperbolicity is exactly the
Laguerre--Polya/RH assertion.  Improving the large-shift asymptotic cannot
cover that ray without a new low-shift theorem, so the proposed two-parameter
patching argument is circular.


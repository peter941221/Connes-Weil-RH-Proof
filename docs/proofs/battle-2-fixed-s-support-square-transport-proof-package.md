## Lemma 1. Fixed-S Projection Transport

Statement:

```text
FixedSProjectionTransport(S,lambda):
  the support and Fourier-support projections used in the canonical fixed-S
  trace are the source projections transported through the CCM comparison
  model, not new cutoffs with reused notation.
```

Proof.

The semilocal support reflection lemma identifies the fixed-S support window
with the transported source window:

```text
P_S(lambda)=S(P_R(lambda)).
```

The Fourier compatibility of the CCM cyclic pair gives the matching
Fourier-support statement:

```text
P_hat_S(lambda)=S(P_hat_R(lambda)).
```

After applying:

```text
V_S=M_S U_S,
```

these become the canonical operators under the positive trace:

```text
P_(S,G)(lambda)=V_S P_S(lambda) V_S^(-1),
P_hat_(S,G)(lambda)=V_S P_hat_S(lambda) V_S^(-1).
```

Thus both projections in:

```text
P_(S,G) P_hat_(S,G) P_(S,G)
```

come from the same source support/Fourier-support pair. The proof does not
replace them by unrelated fixed-S projections.

## Lemma 2. Fixed-S Phase Pullback In One Coordinate

Statement:

```text
FixedSPhasePullback(S,lambda):
  in the common canonical coordinate,
  P_hat_(S,G) is the phase pullback of P_(S,G) by

    u_S=M_S^(-1) I M_S I,

  modulo projection-order defects generated by commuting M_S and M_S^*
  through support or Fourier-support projections.
```

Proof.

Move every operator to the canonical coordinate before forming commutators.
The Fourier reflection in that coordinate gives:

```text
P_hat_(S,G)
  =
u_S^(-1) I P_(S,G) I u_S
```

for the no-defect term.

The only extra terms arise when the finite-S metric factors move across a
support projection or a Fourier-support projection. Each such term contains
one of:

```text
[P,M_S],
[P_hat,M_S],
[P,M_S^*],
[P_hat,M_S^*].
```

All four commutators live in the same coordinate. This removes the coordinate
ambiguity identified in the Battle 2 audit.

## Lemma 3. No-Defect Support-Square Template

Statement:

```text
FixedSNoDefectSupportSquareTemplate(S,I,lambda):
  after the phase pullback, the no-defect part of the fixed-S support-square
  calculation is the CC20 support-square template with u_infty replaced by
  u_S.
```

Proof.

CC20 gives the archimedean template:

```text
P P_hat P
  =
-P(1/2)u_infty^(-1)d^-u_infty P.
```

Lemma 1 identifies the fixed-S support and Fourier-support projections as
transported source projections. Lemma 2 identifies the fixed-S Fourier
projection through the phase:

```text
u_S=M_S^(-1) I M_S I.
```

Apply the same commutator calculation in this one canonical coordinate. The
term with no commutator created by moving `M_S` or `M_S^*` through a projection
is:

```text
-P_(S,G)(1/2)u_S^(-1)d^-u_S P_(S,G).
```

Every term not of this form contains at least one listed projection-order
commutator and is excluded from the no-defect template. This is the point where
the proof replaces the informal phrase `same quantized-calculus expansion` by
a term-by-term split:

```text
fixed-S support square
  =
no-defect u_S template
  +
terms containing M_S or M_S^* projection commutators.
```

## Lemma 4. Defect Classification

Statement:

```text
FixedSDefectClassification(S,I,lambda,J):
  every leftover fixed-S transport term is one of:
    1. no-strip zero-mode rank;
    2. no-strip Tate pole jet;
    3. endpoint-strip Cdef.
```

Proof.

The no-commutator boundary terms are the same finite-dimensional channels as
the source support-square calculation:

```text
hat g(0),
hat g(+i/2),
hat g(-i/2).
```

Battle 1 records them as:

```text
Rank_(S,I)(g),
PoleJetExtra_(S,I)(g).
```

Every non-pure fixed-S transport term contains a projection-order commutator
from Lemma 2. By the projection-defect normal form in
`docs/proofs/semilocal-q-compact-form.md`, such a term is a finite
graph-bounded sum of endpoint-strip shifted kernels:

```text
theta(D^r g) X_0 M_b T_a X_1 theta(D^s g)^*,
```

with `b` supported on a finite endpoint strip. The endpoint-strip trace ideal
then charges it to:

```text
CdefRemainder_(S,I,lambda)(g).
```

No projection-order defect is allowed into the CCM25 `QW_lambda` read-off.

## Lemma 5. Trace-Class And Cyclic Support-Square Legality

Statement:

```text
TraceClassCyclicSupportSquareIdentity(S,I,lambda):
  the theta-smoothed fixed-S support-square terms are trace-class before
  positivity, cyclic trace movement, or CCM read-off is used.
```

Proof.

For the positive trace, define:

```text
A=P_hat_(S,G)(lambda) P_(S,G)(lambda) theta_S(g).
```

The Hilbert-Schmidt gate is:

```text
A in S_2.
```

Then:

```text
PositiveTrace^G_(S,lambda)(g)
  =
Tr(A^*A)
  >=0.
```

For the transported no-defect template, theta smoothing makes the fixed-S
phase summands trace-class in the canonical coordinate. For projection-order
defects, Lemma 4 factors each term through a finite endpoint strip, giving two
Hilbert-Schmidt factors and hence trace-class membership.

Only after these gates can one use ordinary trace cyclicity or compare the
main term with the CCM25 restricted Weil form.

## The Transport Theorem

Combine Lemmas 1 through 5.

The support square splits as:

```text
P_(S,G) P_hat_(S,G) P_(S,G)
  =
-P_(S,G)(1/2)u_S^(-1)d^-u_S P_(S,G)
  +
NoStripBoundaryTerms
  +
ProjectionOrderDefects.
```

After multiplying by `theta_S(g)^*` and `theta_S(g)` and taking the legal
trace:

```text
PositiveTrace^G_(S,lambda)(g)
  =
Tr(theta_S(g)^*
   [-P_(S,G)(1/2)u_S^(-1)d^-u_S P_(S,G)]
   theta_S(g))
  +
Rank_(S,I)(g)
  +
PoleJetExtra_(S,I)(g)
  +
CdefRemainder_(S,I,lambda)(g).
```

The remainder obeys:

```text
|CdefRemainder_(S,I,lambda)(g)|
  <=
C_(S,I,J)(g) Cdef_(S,I,lambda,J)(g).
```

Thus:

```text
FixedSQuantizedSupportSquareTransport(S,I,lambda)
```

holds at route-evidence level. It consumes the Battle 1 ledger
identification and the Battle 3 endpoint-strip `Cdef` norm package, both of
which now have route-evidence proof packages.

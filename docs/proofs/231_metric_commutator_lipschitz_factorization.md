# Proof 231: metric commutator Lipschitz factorization

Date: 2026-07-14

Status: quantitative refinement of Proof 230.  The nested metric correction is
not only in the ideal generated by `[b,u]`; its operator norm is bounded
linearly by the source commutator.  A Schur graph formula separates the two
effects: moving the complement itself, and orthogonalizing it against the
transported `r` range.  Both effects are controlled by the same commutator.
This does not turn the ambient estimate into a root estimate automatically,
but it gives the exact factorization needed for the remaining profile-module
argument.  The integrated sign and RH remain open.  No Lean owner is
authorized.

## 1. Setup

Let `r<=e` be orthogonal projections in a Hilbert space, put

```text
b=e-r,
T=I-alpha u,
h=T* T,
0<=alpha<1,                                           (L.1)
```

and let `r_alpha,e_alpha` be the orthogonal projections onto the transported
ranges.  The nested complement is

```text
b_alpha=e_alpha-r_alpha.                              (L.2)
```

Measure the failure of the source complement to commute with the Euler unitary
by

```text
delta=norm([b,u]).                                     (L.3)
```

Set

```text
m=(1-alpha)^2.                                        (L.4)
```

Every compression of `h` is bounded below by `m`, so its inverse has norm at
most `m^(-1)`.

## 2. The two graph maps

Work in the orthogonal decomposition `e=r direct-sum b`.  Define the metric
blocks

```text
A=r h r,
C=r h b,
D=b h b,
S=D-C* A^(-1) C.                                      (L.5)
```

The Schur formula from Proof 224 gives the exact graph owner

```text
Z=b-r A^(-1) C,
b_alpha=projection onto Ran(T Z).                     (L.6)
```

The simpler metric image of `b` is the projection

```text
q_alpha=projection onto Ran(T b).                      (L.7)
```

The source commutator controls both graph changes.  First,

```text
[b,h]=-alpha([b,u]+[b,u*]),
norm([b,h])<=2alpha delta,
norm(C)=norm(r h b)<=2alpha delta.                     (L.8)
```

Second, with `M=b T b` and `J=(I-b)T b`,

```text
norm(J)=norm((I-b)T b)
 <=alpha delta,
norm(M^(-1))<=1/(1-alpha).                             (L.9)
```

The range `Ran(Tb)` is the graph of

```text
L_b=J M^(-1):Ran(b)->Ran(I-b),
norm(L_b)<=alpha delta/(1-alpha).                      (L.10)
```

For graph subspaces, the norm of the difference between the graph projection
and the base projection is at most the graph norm.  Therefore

```text
norm(q_alpha-b)<=alpha delta/(1-alpha).                (L.11)
```

This is the part that would remain if `r` were absent.

## 3. Orthogonalization against the transported r range

The graph correction in `(L.6)` is

```text
T Z-T b=-T r A^(-1) C.                                 (L.12)
```

Using `norm(T)<=1+alpha`, `(L.8)`, and `norm(A^(-1))<=m^(-1)`,

```text
norm(T Z-T b)
 <=2alpha(1+alpha)delta/m.                             (L.13)
```

Both `T Z` and `T b` are bounded below by `1-alpha` on their source ranges:

```text
norm(T b x)>= (1-alpha)norm(x),
norm(T Z x)>= (1-alpha)norm(x).                        (L.14)
```

The elementary gap estimate for two injective range maps says

```text
norm(proj_Ran(F)-proj_Ran(G))
 <=2 norm(F-G)/min(s_min(F),s_min(G)).                  (L.15)
```

Apply `(L.15)` to `F=T Z`, `G=T b`.  Together with `(L.13)--(L.14)`,

```text
norm(b_alpha-q_alpha)
 <=4alpha(1+alpha)delta/(1-alpha)^3.                  (L.16)
```

Combining `(L.11)` and `(L.16)` gives the explicit Lipschitz estimate

```text
norm(b_alpha-b)
 <=[ alpha/(1-alpha)
    +4alpha(1+alpha)/(1-alpha)^3 ] norm([b,u]).        (L.17)
```

The constant is intentionally coarse; its role is structural, not numerical.
It is finite uniformly for every fixed prime interpolation interval
`alpha<=p^(-1/2)`.

When `[b,u]=0`, `(L.17)` collapses to the exact equality from Proof 230.

## 4. Why this is the right factorization for the root problem

The huge phase and amplitude values seen separately in the finite Sonin
sections are not independent operators.  Their difference is `b_alpha-b`, and
`(L.6)--(L.17)` show that every departure from the source complement is carried
by `C=r h b` or `J=(I-b)T b`, both generated by `[b,u]`.

Thus the remaining root argument has one source object:

```text
source crossing:       [b,u]
metric correction:     bounded graph dressing of [b,u]
post-Q target:          compactness of that dressed crossing.             (L.18)
```

Proofs 228--229 supply the sharp operator-norm treatment for the chirp pieces
of the source crossing.  The surrounding factors in `(L.12)` are bounded
Schur/metric factors.  The only unresolved point is to show that the
finite-window Q-root read-off remains compact under these graph dressings;
ambient norm control `(L.17)` alone is not enough because the read-off is an
unbounded derivative form before cancellation.

## 5. Constructive Neumann compatibility

The blocks in `(L.5)` have the uniform compressed inverse expansion

```text
A^(-1)
 =(1/(1+alpha^2))
   sum_(n>=0)(alpha/(1+alpha^2))^n[r(u+u*)r]^n,         (L.19)
```

with norm ratio `eta=2alpha/(1+alpha^2)<=2sqrt(2)/3`.  Hence any polynomial
growth in the word length of the graph dressing is summable.  Formula
`(L.17)` is the nonperturbative version of that fact: it remains valid without
choosing a word ordering or taking absolute values of the phase and amplitude
separately.

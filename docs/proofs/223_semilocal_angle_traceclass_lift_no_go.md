# Proof 223: semilocal angle trace-class lift no-go

Date: 2026-07-14

Status: exact principal-class obstruction and reproducible numerical guard.
Proof 222 repairs the prime-power coefficients of the half-line metric
projection, but that projection cannot be globalized by simply transporting
CC20's prolate angle correction as a Q-stable trace-class/compact remainder.
The natural finite Euler
positive angle has a nonzero central atom `a^2 log(p)`, while the metric
relative projection has zero central atom.  The mixed archimedean/Euler term
has no Dirac mass, so their difference retains an unbounded post-Q central
form.  A new direct metric-trace theorem is still possible; a trace-class
CC20-angle lift is not.  RH remains unproved.

## 2. Finite Euler positive angle

In one logarithmic translation fiber, the finite Euler scattering phase has
the norm-convergent Laurent series

```text
v_a=sum_m c_m U_(mL),
c_1=-a,
c_(-j)=(1-a^2)a^j,  j>=0.                             (A.4)
```

Let `P` be a half-line projection and `Q=I-P`.  The pure finite-place positive
angle is

```text
B_a=P v_a^* Q v_a P >=0.                              (A.5)
```

For a convolution kernel value `F`, the coefficient of `F(0)` comes from
equal Laurent indices.  At equal support/Fourier cutoffs it is

```text
b_0(a)
 =sum_m |c_m|^2 (-mL)_+
 =(1-a^2)^2 L sum_(j>=1) j a^(2j)
 =a^2 L.                                               (A.6)
```

The last equality uses

```text
sum_(j>=1) j q^j=q/(1-q)^2,
q=a^2.                                                  (A.7)
```

Thus the finite-place positive angle contains the exact central term

```text
a^2 log(p) F(0).                                       (A.8)
```

This is the equal-cutoff specialization of Proof 210, equations
`(A.2)--(A.3)`.  It is also the central Dirac coefficient of the pure
finite-place cocycle computed in Proof 026.

## 3. Metric relative projection has no center

Proof 222 computes the orthogonal half-line metric projection `P_a` onto
`T_a Ran(P)`.  For `Delta_a=P_a-P`, all Laurent diagonal sums are

```text
q_0(Delta_a)=0,
q_k(Delta_a)=-a^|k|,  k!=0.                            (A.9)
```

In particular, the complete metric relative trace has the desired nonzero
prime-power atoms but no `F(0)` term:

```text
Tr(C_F Delta_a)
 =-L sum_(m>=1)a^m(F(mL)+F(-mL)).                      (A.10)
```

This zero is not an asymptotic cancellation.  Both `P_a` and `P` have the
same fiber rank, and the exact graph-projection blocks cancel at translation
degree zero.

Combining `(A.6)` and `(A.9)` gives the unavoidable principal mismatch

```text
central(B_a)-central(Delta_a)=a^2 L.                   (A.11)
```

## 4. The mixed term cannot cancel it

For the full semilocal phase `u_infinity v_a`, the exact quantized cocycle
splits as

```text
D_(u_infinity v_a)
 =D_(v_a)+D_(u_infinity)+Cross_(u_infinity,v_a).       (A.12)
```

Proof 026 derives this identity and proves the relevant singularity
separation.  The archimedean coefficient is piecewise smooth with cusps, and
the geometrically summable translations in the mixed term preserve local
integrability.  Hence

```text
Cross_(u_infinity,v_a) has no Dirac mass.               (A.13)
```

After applying the support-preserving operator

```text
Q_+=-partial_b^2+1/4,
```

the mixed term gives a compact fixed-window kernel.  It cannot cancel the
second-derivative point interaction generated by `(A.8)`.

Therefore any decomposition of the full finite-S positive angle relative to
the metric Sonin projection retains

```text
a^2 L [-F''(0)+(1/4)F(0)]                              (A.14)
```

inside its purported angle correction.

## 5. Post-Q noncompactness certificate

Let

```text
xi_t(x)=exp(itx) phi(x),
||phi||_2=1,
```

with `phi` a fixed real smooth test.  For
`F_t=xi_t*xi_t^*`, integration by parts gives

```text
-F_t''(0)+(1/4)F_t(0)
 =||xi_t'||_2^2+(1/4)||xi_t||_2^2
 =t^2+||phi'||_2^2+1/4.                               (A.15)
```

The normalized vectors `xi_t` converge weakly to zero as `|t|` tends to
infinity.  A compact quadratic form tends to zero on this sequence, whereas
the contribution `(A.14)` grows as

```text
a^2 log(p) t^2.                                        (A.16)
```

Thus the difference between the natural finite-S positive angle and the
metric relative projection is not a Q-stable trace-class/compact angle
correction.  In particular, CC20's archimedean `K_prol` cannot be transported
to finite S by adding only a trace-class perturbation after assigning the
metric prime-power series.

## 6. What remains possible

This no-go does not reject the endpoint metric projection itself.  It rejects
one proposed proof of its trace formula:

```text
invalid route:
  semilocal positive angle
    - transported trace-class CC20 angle correction
    = metric Sonin projection;

still open:
  compute Tr(C_g R_a C_g^*) directly,
  identify its complete finite-prime series using the metric telescoping,
  construct its own post-Q remainder on the convolution-root Hilbert space,
  prove that remainder's sign.                          (A.17)
```

CCM24 proves the Sonin-space isomorphism but supplies no finite-S analogue of
CC20's trace-class angle decomposition.  Equation `(A.11)` shows that such an
analogue, if formulated for the natural semilocal positive angle, must contain
a noncompact central sector rather than a transported trace-class correction.

# Proof 230: metric nested-complement ideal invariance

Date: 2026-07-14

Status: exact quotient-algebra theorem.  For nested projections `r<=e`, put
`b=e-r`.  Under the Euler metric transport `T_alpha=I-alpha u`, the orthogonal
nested complement `b_alpha=e_alpha-r_alpha` differs from `b` by an element of
the closed two-sided ideal generated by the single source commutator `[b,u]`.
Thus the compressed inverse, Schur complement, and oblique-defect amplitude
cannot create a new essential operator class.  To finish the project route one
must still prove that the post-`Q` root-compact class from Proofs 228--229 is
stable under the surrounding semilocal scattering words.  This theorem alone
does not prove that stability, the three-row sign, or RH.  No Lean owner is
authorized.

## 1. Metric image of a projection

Let `A` be a unital `C*`-algebra and let `u` be a unitary element.  For

```text
0<=alpha<1,
T=I-alpha u,
h=T* T,                                                (I.1)
```

one has

```text
(1-alpha)^2 I<=h<=(1+alpha)^2 I.                      (I.2)
```

If `p` is a projection, then `php` is positive and invertible in the corner
algebra `pAp`.  Define

```text
Phi_T(p)=T p(php)^(-1)p T*.                            (I.3)
```

Formula `(I.3)` is a projection.  In a faithful Hilbert-space representation
it is the orthogonal projection onto `T Ran(p)`.

Let `r,e` be projections with

```text
r<=e,
b=e-r.                                                 (I.4)
```

Then `b` is a projection orthogonal to `r`.  Since `T` is invertible,
`Phi_T(r)<=Phi_T(e)`.  Put

```text
r_alpha=Phi_T(r),
e_alpha=Phi_T(e),
b_alpha=e_alpha-r_alpha.                              (I.5)
```

The problem left by Proof 229 is whether metric orthogonalization can insert
an essential channel into `b_alpha-b` that was absent from the normalized
source complement.

## 2. Exact commuting theorem

Assume first that

```text
[b,u]=0.                                               (I.6)
```

Then `b` commutes with `T`, `T*`, and `h`.  Since `rb=0`,

```text
r h b=0,
b h r=0.                                               (I.7)
```

Thus the compression of `h` to `e=r+b` is exactly block diagonal:

```text
e h e=r h r+b h b.                                    (I.8)
```

Its corner inverse splits into the two orthogonal corners,

```text
(e h e)^(-1)
 =(r h r)^(-1)+(b h b)^(-1).                          (I.9)
```

Substitute `(I.9)` into `(I.3)`:

```text
e_alpha
 =r_alpha+T b(b h b)^(-1)b T*.                        (I.10)
```

Because `b` commutes with `h`, the inverse in the `b` corner is `b h^(-1)b`.
Also

```text
T h^(-1)T*
 =T(T* T)^(-1)T*
 =I.                                                   (I.11)
```

Equations `(I.10)--(I.11)` give the exact result

```text
b_alpha=e_alpha-r_alpha=b.                            (I.12)
```

The subspace `Ran(r)` may move nontrivially.  The conclusion only says that a
commuting orthogonal complement stays fixed and remains orthogonal to the
transported `r` range.  No expansion in `alpha` is involved.

## 3. Quotient by an arbitrary ideal

Let `J` be any closed two-sided ideal of `A`, and let

```text
pi:A -> A/J                                             (I.13)
```

be the quotient `*`-homomorphism.  Functional calculus, corner inverses, and
formula `(I.3)` commute with `pi`; the uniform lower bound `(I.2)` keeps every
corner inverse valid after quotienting.

Assume

```text
[b,u] belongs to J.                                   (I.14)
```

Then `pi(b)` and `pi(u)` commute.  Apply the exact theorem `(I.12)` inside
`A/J`.  It gives

```text
pi(b_alpha)=pi(b),                                    (I.15)
```

or equivalently

```text
b_alpha-b belongs to J.                               (I.16)
```

Take `J_[b,u]` to be the closed two-sided ideal generated by `[b,u]`.  Since
`[b,u]` is zero in the corresponding quotient, `(I.16)` yields the
unconditional ideal-containment theorem

```text
b_alpha-b belongs to Ideal([b,u]).                    (I.17)
```

This is the exact algebraic owner of the nonconstant metric correction.

## 4. What happened to the compressed inverses

Proof 226 displays the amplitude through compressed inverses and a Schur
complement.  Those formulas contain many words, but `(I.17)` proves a stronger
statement about their sum:

```text
every term surviving in b_alpha-b contains, after ideal reduction,
at least one copy of the source failure [b,u].         (I.18)
```

Indeed, setting `[b,u]=0` annihilates the complete correction exactly by
`(I.12)`.  In noncommutative algebra this is precisely the statement that the
correction belongs to the ideal generated by the commutator.

Consequently none of the following can create a new quotient class:

```text
the inverse (p h p)^(-1);
the Schur complement inverse;
the same-range oblique defect square;
the Kato re-orthogonalization of the nested complement.                   (I.19)
```

They can only dress the source crossing on the left and right by bounded
words.

## 5. Application boundary for the RH route

The actual post-`Q` root construction is not itself a `*`-homomorphism from
all bounded ambient operators: it takes a smoothed trace diagonal and then a
finite-window compression.  Therefore `(I.17)` must not be cited alone as a
proof of root compactness.

What Proof 230 changes is the remaining target.  There is no independent
"metric amplitude essential spectrum" to estimate.  It is enough to prove
that the root-compact profile class containing the source commutator is stable
under the bounded words that generate `Ideal([b,u])` in this semilocal
scattering algebra.

Proofs 228--229 already provide the candidate class:

```text
locally L2 interiors with absolutely summable HS norms

plus logarithmic-chirp boundaries with summable operator norms.            (I.20)
```

The corrected final compactness contract is:

```text
show that class (I.20) is stable under

  Euler multipliers U^k,
  the two half-line projections,
  archimedean scattering conjugation,
  and the rapidly summable prolate correction.          (I.21)
```

Once `(I.21)` is proved, `(I.17)` transports Proof 229's source compactness
through the full metric orthogonalization.  Until then, full metric
compactness remains open.

## 6. Quantitative perturbation interpretation

The quotient theorem is qualitative, but it also explains small finite
perturbations.  If `u_epsilon` tends in norm to a unitary commuting with `b`,
then

```text
norm([b,u_epsilon])->0.                               (I.22)
```

All operations in `(I.3)` are norm continuous under the uniform lower bound
`(I.2)`, so

```text
norm(b_(alpha,epsilon)-b)->0.                         (I.23)
```

The companion certificate tests this behavior.  The route proof uses the
exact ideal statement `(I.17)`, not a perturbative estimate.

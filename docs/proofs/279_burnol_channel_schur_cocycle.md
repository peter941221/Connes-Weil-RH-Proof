# Proof 279: Burnol channel Schur cocycle

Date: 2026-07-15

Status: exact finite-dimensional channel factorization and mixed-derivative
readback.  Burnol's plus/minus Fourier channels orthogonally split the static
boundary Gram, but a perturbed boundary determinant also contains an
off-diagonal Schur coupling.  That coupling is generated by the Fourier-odd
part of the perturbation, equivalently by a Fourier commutator.  A deterministic
three-dimensional model has `F_0=K_prol=0` and relative covariance `-1/4`, so
neither the direct channels nor their coupling may be discarded.

This identifies the complete finite boundary cocycle which a continuous
source-relative theorem must preserve.  It does not construct that continuous
determinant, prove its root-sandwiched trace domain, prove the half-power bound,
close Gate 3U, or prove RH.

## 2. Normalized Burnol channels

Let `J:H_0 -> H` be the source-window inclusion, so `P_0=J J*`.  Let `F` be
the self-adjoint even Fourier transform and put

```text
F_0=J* F J.                                                   (AP.2)
```

Proof 278 uses `A(u,v)=Ju+FJv`.  Apply the block Hadamard transform and define

```text
A_+=(J+FJ)/sqrt(2),       G_+=A_+*A_+=I+F_0,
A_-=(J-FJ)/sqrt(2),       G_-=A_-*A_-=I-F_0.          (AP.3)
```

The cross Gram vanishes:

```text
A_+*A_-=0.                                                   (AP.4)
```

Normalize the two synthesis maps:

```text
V_+=A_+ G_+^(-1/2),       C_+=V_+V_+*,
V_-=A_- G_-^(-1/2),       C_-=V_-V_-*.               (AP.5)
```

Then

```text
V_+*V_+=V_-*V_-=I,       V_+*V_-=0,
F V_+=V_+,               F V_-=-V_-,
I-R=C_++C_-.                                             (AP.6)
```

Burnol's Theorem 4 supplies the projection formula behind `(AP.3)--(AP.6)`.
Its Corollary 5 gives the separate `(1+F_0)^(-1)` and `(1-F_0)^(-1)` formulas
on Fourier-invariant and Fourier-anti-invariant inputs.  These are static
boundary statements; they do not yet diagonalize a detector perturbation.

## 3. The perturbed boundary has a coupling block

Let `M` be positive and invertible.  Proof 278's normalized complementary
boundary matrix is

```text
B_M=
 [[D_+, X],
  [X*,  D_-]],                                             (AP.7)

D_+=V_+* M^(-1) V_+,
D_-=V_-* M^(-1) V_-,
X  =V_+* M^(-1) V_-.                                      (AP.8)
```

At `M=I`, `D_+=D_-=I` and `X=0`.  Away from the base, the Hadamard
diagonalization of `G` does not imply `X=0`.

This separates two notions which must not be conflated:

```text
+----------------------+-----------------------------------------------+
| object               | what it measures                              |
+----------------------+-----------------------------------------------+
| F_0=J*FJ             | static overlap of the two source boundaries   |
| [F,M^(-1)]           | Fourier parity broken by the perturbation     |
+----------------------+-----------------------------------------------+
```

Indeed, with

```text
M_even=(M^(-1)+F M^(-1)F)/2,
M_odd =(M^(-1)-F M^(-1)F)/2,                           (AP.9)
```

the blocks satisfy

```text
D_+=V_+*M_even V_+,
D_-=V_-*M_even V_-,
X  =V_+*M_odd V_-
   =(1/2)V_+*[F,M^(-1)]V_-.                           (AP.10)
```

Thus the coupling is already a completed two-point difference.  If `M`
commutes with `F`, it vanishes.  The route multipliers do not come with that
commutation theorem, so setting it to zero would add a false premise.

## 4. Exact Schur cocycle

Define the normalized Schur factor

```text
Omega_M
 =D_-^(-1/2)
   (D_- -X*D_+^(-1)X)
  D_-^(-1/2).                                           (AP.11)
```

Positivity of `M` makes `B_M` and its Schur complement positive.  In finite
dimension,

```text
det(B_M)=det(D_+)det(D_-)det(Omega_M).                  (AP.12)
```

Proof 278's ambient-free relative Jacobi identity therefore becomes

```text
det_E(E M E)/det_R(R M R)
 =det_(H_0)(J* M^(-1)J)
  /[det(D_+)det(D_-)det(Omega_M)].                     (AP.13)
```

Equation `(AP.13)` is an exact finite-dimensional factorization of one
relative determinant.  It does not assert that its four displayed
determinants exist separately on the continuous carrier.  In particular, the
Euler multiplier is still not an identity-plus-trace-class operator.

## 5. Mixed derivative readback

For an orthogonal projection `Q`, define the Toeplitz covariance

```text
S_Q(W,H)=Tr(Q W(I-Q)H Q).                              (AP.14)
```

Take commuting self-adjoint `W,H` and

```text
M_(s,t)=exp(sW+tH).                                    (AP.15)
```

At the base, the first jets of the coupling block are

```text
partial_s X|_0=-V_+*W V_-,
partial_t X|_0=-V_+*H V_-.                            (AP.16)
```

Put

```text
kappa(W,H)
 =2 Re Tr(C_- W C_+ H C_-).                           (AP.17)
```

Differentiating the four factors in `(AP.13)` gives

```text
partial_(s,t) log det(D_+)|_0     =S_(C_+)(W,H),
partial_(s,t) log det(D_-)|_0     =S_(C_-)(W,H),
partial_(s,t) log det(Omega)|_0   =-kappa(W,H).        (AP.18)
```

Consequently

```text
S_(I-R)
 =S_(C_+)+S_(C_-)-kappa,                              (AP.19)

S_E-S_R
 =S_(P_0)-S_(C_+)-S_(C_-)+kappa.                     (AP.20)
```

The complement equalities `S_(P_0)=S_E` and `S_(I-R)=S_R` use commutation of
`W,H` and the same completed trace-legality contract as Proof 277.  Equation
`(AP.20)` is the exact derivative readback: two direct channels and the Schur
coupling are all part of the route scalar.

## 6. Exact zero-prolate guard

The rejection can be made deterministic on `C^3`.  Let

```text
F=[[0,1,0],
   [1,0,0],
   [0,0,1]],

P_0=projection onto e_1,
v=(e_2+e_3)/sqrt(2),
W=H=v v*.                                              (AP.21)
```

Here `F_0=0`.  The Burnol complement is `span(e_1,e_2)`, the Sonin space is
`span(e_3)`, and

```text
K_prol=E E_hat E-R=0.                                 (AP.22)
```

Direct calculation gives

```text
S_(C_+)=3/16,
S_(C_-)=3/16,
kappa=1/8,
S_R=1/4,
S_E=0,
S_E-S_R=-1/4.                                         (AP.23)
```

Dropping the coupling changes the relative answer from `-1/4` to `-3/8`.
Keeping only the coupling gives `+1/8`.  Both failures occur with a positive
rank-one detector and with the prolate correction identically zero.  This is
an algebraic rejection, not a discretization claim about the continuous
Fourier kernel.

## 7. Analytic consequence

Proof 279 changed the next target.  Proof 280 subsequently constructed the
complete root-sandwiched relative cocycle whose finite shadow is `(AP.13)`, and
Proof 281 descended it to one ordinary band Fredholm determinant.  The
remaining problem is the uniform estimate, not the determinant domain.  The
channel requirements below remain the readback contract for those successors.

There are two source mechanisms to exploit, but they must be recombined before
the final absolute value:

```text
diagonal relative channel:
  J*M^(-1)J versus the two Fourier-even blocks D_+,D_-;

coupling channel:
  X=(1/2)V_+*[F,M^(-1)]V_-, hence an exact two-point difference.   (AP.24)
```

The coupling formula is promising because compact-root support acts directly
on a commutator kernel.  It is not sufficient: `(AP.23)` proves that the
diagonal relative channel can survive without `F_0` or `K_prol`.

The next continuous theorem must therefore satisfy all of the following:

```text
1. start from Proof 267's relative determinant line, not det(M);

2. insert the compact root before claiming trace-class membership;

3. retain the diagonal relative channel and Omega coupling together;

4. differentiate back to both outer orientations, the second-support term,
   and the prolate commutator;

5. keep the normalized Euler product whole through mixed-prime resummation;

6. take one absolute value only after the signed scalar trace.             (AP.25)
```

A useful first analytic lemma is a polynomial-support Schatten estimate for
the root-sandwiched commutator block in `(AP.24)`.  It is only one leg of the
theorem.  The harder leg is a same-object cancellation for the diagonal
relative channel.

## 10. Route judgment

Proof 279 closes the finite channel-ownership question.  The complete relative
boundary determinant has two diagonal channels and one nonlinear Schur
coupling.  The coupling is a Fourier commutator, but the whole response is not
divisible by the static truncated-Fourier or prolate factor.

The successor chain is now

```text
Proof 280 root-sandwiched relative cocycle
  -> Proof 281 ordinary band Fredholm determinant
  -> signed outer-band minus Sonin-band crossings
  -> real-line common-path cancellation
  -> compact displacement stopping
  -> uniform polynomial-support bound
  -> Gate 3U.                                             (AP.26)
```

The source-relative BOGC factorization, uniform estimate, complete prime
stopping/telescope, Gate 3U, the finite-S sign, the arithmetic same-object
trace identity, negative-owner integration, Burnol's all-zero identity, and
RH remain open.  No Lean owner or route consumer is changed.

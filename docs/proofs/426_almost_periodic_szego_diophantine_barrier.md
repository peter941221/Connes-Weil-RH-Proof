## 2. Contract of the available theorem

Zhou's uniform almost-periodic determinant theorem studies finite sections of
operators with almost-periodic diagonals.  For a finite section
`[n_1,n_2)`, its normalized determinant has the form

```text
det(P_(n_1,n_2) A P_(n_1,n_2))/G^(n_2-n_1)

 =Theta_(A,1)(tau_(n_1)) Theta_(A,2)(tau_(n_2))+o(1),
   n_2-n_1 ->infinity.                                (AP.2)
```

Here `tau_n` is the phase character of the frequency group and both `Theta`
functions live on the compact character group `Hom(Xi,T)`.  Formula `(AP.2)`
is the uniform version of the fractal-sequence asymptotic.  A fixed constant
appears only after one fixes endpoint phase subsequences.

For a finitely generated group, Theorem 4.1.9 uses an admissible compatible
weight.  Proposition 4.1.6 constructs the polynomial weight

```text
beta(alpha)=(1+abs(alpha))^omega                       (AP.3)
```

from a lower bound

```text
norm(sum_(j=1)^n alpha_j xi_j)_(R/Z)
 >=C(1+max_j abs(alpha_j))^(-omega)                   (AP.4)
```

for nonzero integer vectors `alpha`.  Example 4.1.8 invokes the
Baker--Feldman theorem for logarithms of algebraic numbers.

The theorem places its smoothness and inverse-closed algebra assumptions in a
weighted Wiener norm built from `beta`.  A Gate 3U application would need one
choice whose order and constants remain polynomial in the root support while
the visible prime set changes.

## 3. Prime logarithms give independent torus frequencies

For distinct primes `p_1,...,p_n`, define

```text
xi_j=log(p_j)/(2 pi) mod Z.                            (AP.5)
```

The numbers `1,xi_1,...,xi_n` are linearly independent over `Q`.  Suppose

```text
k_0+sum_j k_j xi_j=0                                  (AP.6)
```

with integer coefficients.  Multiplying by `2 pi` and exponentiating gives

```text
product_j p_j^(k_j)=exp(-2 pi k_0).                   (AP.7)
```

If `k_0=0`, unique factorization forces every `k_j=0`.  If `k_0` is nonzero,
the left side is algebraic while the right side is transcendental.  The last
claim follows from Gelfond--Schneider applied to a value of
`(-1)^(-2 i k_0)=exp(2 pi k_0)`.  Hence `(AP.6)` has only the zero solution.

In particular,

```text
norm(sum_j alpha_j xi_j)_(R/Z)>0                      (AP.8)
```

for each nonzero `alpha in Z^n`.  A compatible lower bound can exist for each
fixed finite family, but its exponent must reflect the number of generators.

## 4. Dimension lower bound for every compatible exponent

Let `xi_1,...,xi_n in R/Z` satisfy `(AP.8)`.  Assume constants `C>0` and
`omega>=0` obey

```text
norm(alpha dot xi)_(R/Z)
 >=C(1+norm(alpha)_infinity)^(-omega)                 (AP.9)
```

for every nonzero integer vector `alpha`.

Fix an integer `Q>=1`.  The box

```text
{0,...,Q}^n                                           (AP.10)
```

contains `(Q+1)^n` vectors.  Place the corresponding points
`alpha dot xi` on the unit circle.  The circular gaps between their ordered
values have total length one, so two points have circular distance at most

```text
(Q+1)^(-n).                                          (AP.11)
```

Subtract their box vectors.  Independence makes the difference
`gamma` nonzero, and

```text
norm(gamma)_infinity<=Q,
norm(gamma dot xi)_(R/Z)<=(Q+1)^(-n).                 (AP.12)
```

Combining `(AP.9)` and `(AP.12)` yields

```text
C(Q+1)^(-omega)<=(Q+1)^(-n),
C<=(Q+1)^(omega-n).                                  (AP.13)
```

If `omega<n`, the right side tends to zero as `Q` grows, contradicting
`C>0`.  Every compatible polynomial exponent satisfies

```text
omega>=n.                                            (AP.14)
```

This proof uses no estimate from transcendence theory after independence has
been established.  Baker--Feldman supplies a fixed-family upper existence
result; `(AP.14)` supplies the dimension lower bound that a uniform route must
pay.

## 5. Canonical root support can make `n` exponential

The support-coupled finite set from Proof 416 need not be sparse.  Choose a
nonnegative smooth root `g_B`, supported in `[-B,B]` and positive on the open
interval.  Its autocorrelation

```text
F_B(z)=integral_R g_B(t)g_B(t+z)dt                   (AP.15)
```

satisfies

```text
F_B(z)>0,  abs(z)<2B.                                (AP.16)
```

For every prime `p<exp(2B)`, both `F_B(log p)` and `F_B(-log p)` are positive.
The exact-support family therefore sees every such prime:

```text
{p prime:p<exp(2B)} subset S_(F_B).                  (AP.17)
```

The prime number theorem gives

```text
card(S_(F_B))
 >=pi(exp(2B))
 ~exp(2B)/(2B).                                      (AP.18)
```

Insert `(AP.18)` into `(AP.14)`.  The compatible exponent required by the
standard finitely generated almost-periodic theorem can grow at least as

```text
omega_B>=pi(exp(2B)).                                (AP.19)
```

Proof 416 controls the diagonal Euler energy by `O((1+B)^2)`.  It gives no
bound of the form `(AP.19)` for a weighted Wiener norm, nor should it: the

## 6. Endpoint phases reproduce the cutoff anomaly

Formula `(AP.2)` keeps separate functions of the left and right endpoint
characters.  For the prime-log group,

```text
tau_m(xi_p)=exp(2 pi i m xi_p)=exp(i m log p).        (AP.20)
```

Rational independence makes the integer orbit dense in the corresponding
finite prime torus by Kronecker's theorem.  Different cutoff subsequences can
therefore approach different endpoint phases, and `(AP.2)` can return
different boundary constants.

Zhou also records that the differentiability of the functions `Theta_(A,k)`
with respect to the torus coordinates is open.  Proof 416 needs a detector
gradient, not only existence of a continuous boundary factor.  Thus the
source theorem leaves both required steps outside its contract:

```text
select the physical normal-ordering endpoint;
bound its root-relative derivative uniformly in S_F. (AP.21)
```

Proof 425 gives an operator-level version of the same issue.  Translation-
covariant and detector-invisible equal-rank padding have identical strong
projection endpoints but different detector-trace limits.  The phase factors
in `(AP.2)` retain precisely the endpoint information that strong convergence
forgets.

## 7. Why the fixed-`S` theorem does not close Gate 3U

For one fixed finite `S`, Baker--Feldman can supply a compatible weight, and a
fractal cutoff subsequence can select endpoint phases.  That fixed-family
statement has constants depending on:

```text
the rank and Diophantine data of Xi_S;
the two selected endpoint phase limits;
the weighted Wiener norms and inverse data of the operator.        (AP.22)
```

Gate 3U requires constants independent of the canonical visible set after a
fixed polynomial dependence on `B_F` and a fixed Sobolev order.  Equations
`(AP.14)--(AP.19)` prevent the cited almost-periodic theorem from supplying
that uniformity.  Equation `(AP.2)` also retains a cutoff-dependent scalar
whose detector derivative the theorem does not estimate.

This conclusion does not extend to every possible almost-periodic method.  It
applies to the existing compatible-weight strong Szego--Widom route and any
argument that hides the same small denominators inside a fixed polynomial
weight.

# Plan 019 H2 Mathematical Verdict

Date: 2026-07-11

Verdict: **rejected at the H2/H3 interface.** The CC20 trace remainder cannot
be the uncancelled exact/coexact contribution of a scaling-equivariant positive
Hilbert complex. If the differential is scaling-equivariant, exact and coexact
contributions pair and cancel in the supertrace. If the fixed support/Fourier
cutoffs are used to reproduce the CC20 kernel, the differential is not
scaling-equivariant. Splitting off the finitely many bad spectral directions of
`-2 Id + K_I` merely reproduces CC20's existing finite-codimension
conditioning; it is not a new cohomological producer.

This verdict rejects Plan 019 as a guaranteed RH route in its proposed form. It
does not reject the possibility of a future arithmetic cohomology built from
different lower geometry.

## 1. Exact CC20 Object

For a symmetric bounded interval `I`, CC20 Theorem `thmqkey1` proves

```text
D o Q(xi * xi*)
  = <xi, (-2 Id + K_I) xi>,
```

on `L2(sqrt(I), d*rho)`. In logarithmic coordinates,

```text
Q_+ = -partial_x^2 + 1/4,

Q_+ delta(exp(|x|))
  = -2 Dirac_0 + regular even function.
```

The compact part has the symmetric Hilbert--Schmidt kernel

```text
k_I(v,u) = Q delta(max(u/v,v/u)).
```

Source:

```text
Connes--Consani, Weil positivity and trace formula, the archimedean place
https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.13771
weil-compo.tex:755-808.
```

The same source identifies the pre-`Q` small-square coefficient as

```text
delta(rho)
  = Tr(rep(rho^-1) P_hat_1 P_1),
```

where `P_1` and `P_hat_1` are the support and Fourier-support projections.
Their positive compression has prolate eigenvalues

```text
P_1 P_hat_1 P_1 xi_n = lambda(n)^2 xi_n,
0 < lambda(n)^2 < 1,
```

and is not a projection. See `weil-compo.tex:884-922,926-983,997-1006`.

## 2. What A Hilbert-Complex Boundary Can Contribute

Let

```text
C0 --d--> C1
```

be a closed Hilbert complex and let `U` be the scaling action. For the harmonic
sector to carry the intended representation, Plan 019 H3 requires

```text
U_1 d = d U_0.
```

Then `U` preserves `closure(range d)` and its orthogonal complement. On the
nonzero singular-value sectors, `d` pairs the exact contribution in `C1` with
the corresponding coexact contribution in `C0`. Whenever the smoothed traces
are legal, cyclicity gives

```text
Tr(U_1 d d* F(d d*)) = Tr(U_0 d* d F(d* d)).
```

Thus the two adjacent degrees cancel in the alternating supertrace. This is the
standard mechanism by which a Hilbert-complex supertrace localizes to harmonic
cohomology.

There are only two possibilities:

```text
equivariant d:
  boundary sectors cancel and leave no K_I remainder;

non-equivariant d:
  a commutator/cutoff defect can remain, but the harmonic quotient is not a
  scaling representation and the Lefschetz/Hodge argument is unavailable.
```

The proposed mechanism needs both an uncancelled `K_I` and equivariance. These
requirements are incompatible.

## 3. Kernel Mismatch For The Natural Cutoff Candidate

The CC20 coefficient is generated by the product

```text
P_hat_1 P_1,
```

not by an orthogonal projection onto a closed exact range. Its compression has
the non-idempotent spectrum `lambda(n)^2` displayed above. An exact-sector
projection has spectrum contained in `{0,1}`.

One can manufacture a differential such as

```text
d = P_hat_1 P_1
```

so that `d* d` contains the prolate compression. This does not pass H0/H3:
support projection `P_1` is tied to the fixed cutoff and scaling moves its
support window. Hence it does not commute with the full scaling action. The
failure of this commutation is precisely the boundary/small-square phenomenon
that produced the remainder.

Alternatively, one can define

```text
d* d := 2 Id - K_I
```

on a subspace where the right side is nonnegative. This is forbidden by H0:
the differential has been selected by functional calculus after seeing the
target remainder. It stores rather than explains the desired identity.

## 4. Sign Obstruction

A contribution of the form

```text
-<d xi, d xi>
```

has a fixed nonpositive sign. CC20 proves that the actual quadratic form is not
sign-definite on the relevant larger window. In Remark `improve` it constructs
a positive-definite test supported in `[-log 2,log 2]` with

```text
D_+(Q_+ f_epsilon) -> 2.98699 > 0.
```

See `weil-compo.tex:832-870`. On the other hand, since `K_I` is compact,
`-2 Id + K_I` is negative on an infinite-dimensional tail. Therefore the
actual remainder cannot be one signed boundary norm.

The positive part is finite-dimensional. Declaring those bad eigenvectors to
be `H0` or `H2`, and using the negative complement as a boundary sector, is
formally possible but circular at the ownership level:

```text
the eigenspaces depend on K_I and the support window I;
they are selected after diagonalizing the defect;
their orthogonality conditions are exactly CC20's finite-codimension
conditioning mechanism.
```

It supplies no new all-tests positivity and does not remove the detector/
conditioning problem already rejected in Plans 016 and 012.

## 5. Compactness Guard

The earlier Plan 012 verdict supplies an independent guard. On the
codimension-one, infinite-dimensional space

```text
H_0 = {xi | integral xi d*rho = 0},
```

vanishing of the remainder would imply by polarization

```text
compression_H0(K_I) = 2 Id_H0.
```

The left side is compact and the right side is not. Therefore the remainder
does not disappear under the route's natural moment boundary condition. See
`docs/proofs/cc20-012-mathematical-verdict.md:230-265`.

## 6. Gate Decision

```text
+--------------------------------------+-----------------------------------+
| requirement                          | result                            |
+--------------------------------------+-----------------------------------+
| exact CC20 kernel extracted          | yes                               |
| natural cutoff product is projection | no; prolate eigenvalues in (0,1)  |
| fixed-sign boundary norm matches     | no; remainder is sign-indefinite  |
| equivariant exact sectors leave K_I  | no; adjacent degrees cancel       |
| cutoff differential equivariant      | no; scaling moves the cutoff      |
| finite bad sector is a new producer  | no; it is old conditioning        |
+--------------------------------------+-----------------------------------+
```

Final result:

```text
H2 literal kernel ownership: rejected.
H3 compatibility: incompatible with the proposed H2 realization.
Plan 019: rejected in its present form.
RH status: still conditional.
Lean action: none; no theorem-grade route API exists.
```

The reusable lesson is narrower than “cohomology cannot prove RH.” A future
cohomological route must construct its complex before introducing a support
cutoff and must obtain the explicit formula as a genuine equivariant
supertrace. Regrading a known non-equivariant cutoff remainder after the fact
does not do this.
